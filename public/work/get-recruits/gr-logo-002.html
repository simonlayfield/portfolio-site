The Process

Contrary to the brief, I decided that there were some compelling reasons for developing a version of the first concept using the image of a football. The challenge in this case is to avoid a generic, tacky look. Hopefully I have succeeded in avoiding this and in doing so have maintained a fresh approach.

The important thing to remember when assessing this logo is that ultimately the goal is to create a well-defined, memorable, purposeful brand image. I feel that this logo acheives these points. It's simple, easy to understand from a distance or at a quick glance - and I think that is mainly down to the use of the football.

I also decided to give this version more of a weathered, gritty look by including a speckled, eroded effect. I think this adds personality to the logo and helps to break up the simplicity of the shape just enough to give it a unique look.

Layout/Application

Ensuring that the logo is suited to a number of different applications is an important consideration when developing a concept. Where and how the logo is seen may not always be ideal, but strong, well-balanced visual elements should always successfully deliver your visual message, even when resized or rearranged.
